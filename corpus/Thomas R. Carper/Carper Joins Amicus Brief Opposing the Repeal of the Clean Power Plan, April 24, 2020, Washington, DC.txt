Carper Joins Amicus Brief Opposing the Repeal of the Clean Power Plan


Today, U.S. Senator Tom Carper (D-Del.), top Democrat on the Senate Environment and Public Works Committee, joined U.S. Representatives Paul D. Tonko (D-N.Y.-20) and Jared Huffman (D-Calif.-02) and more than 70 members of Congress in an amicus brief to the U.S. Court of Appeals for the D.C. Circuit opposing the Environmental Protection Agency's (EPA) replacement of the Obama Administration's Clean Power Plan (CPP) with the Trump Administration's so-called Affordable Clean Energy (ACE) Rule.

The brief asserts that EPA acted well within its broad authority under the Clean Air Act to issue the Clean Power Plan and rejects its repeal by the Trump Administration.

U.S. Senators Michael Bennet (D-Colo.), Ben Cardin (D-Md.), Dianne Feinstein (D-Calif.), Edward J. Markey (D-Mass.), and Chris Van Hollen (D-Md.) signed the brief.
